FAQ

The questions worth asking.

Is this against YouTube's rules?

No. YouChop renders chapters locally in your browser — nothing is posted, automated, or modified on YouTube itself. If you choose to share timestamps in a comment, you paste them yourself, manually, like any other comment. YouChop never auto-posts anything, anywhere.

What does the extension read?

Only the public transcript of the video you're currently watching. No browsing history, no personal data collection, minimal permissions (youtube.com only). Our privacy policy states exactly what chapter data we store and how to request deletion.

How does the creator back-catalog service access my videos?

With your permission, through YouTube's official API — you sign in with Google and consent to caption access for your own channel. Nothing is scraped, and you can revoke access anytime. Your chapters are delivered as editable text you paste into your descriptions: they're yours forever, even if you never use YouChop again.

What happens when I run out of credits?

You can earn more free (share chapters +1, invite a friend +10) or buy a pack. And any video someone has already chapterized loads instantly and never costs you a credit.

Do the chapters appear for other people?

Any YouChop user opening that video sees them instantly — it's already chopped and cached. Want everyone to benefit? Copy the timestamps and paste them into the comments, or send them to the creator to add to the description.

Does it work on every video?

It needs the video to have a transcript or captions, which almost all long-form YouTube videos do. Rare videos without any captions can't be chapterized — we'll tell you before spending a credit.
