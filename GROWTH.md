# YouChop — Growth

*Two loops. One makes money, one makes installs. Both are compliance-first: YouChop NEVER auto-posts to YouTube. Ever.*

## Primary loop — the creator sample loop (revenue)

```
pick target channel → chop 3–5 of their most-viewed unchaptered videos (COGS $0.05–0.15)
→ email: "here are free chapters for your top videos — want the other 195 done for $99?"
→ creator pastes chapters into DESCRIPTIONS (where a clickable youchop.app credit IS allowed)
→ pack sale → $12/mo continuity sub → lead list compounds
```

- Starts **now**, with manual fulfillment — needs no bulk infrastructure.
- **Sample-only pre-sale is a hard budget rule:** max 5 videos/channel, 150 videos/week founder cap. Full-catalog demos would burn $160–440/month for negative-margin sales.
- Volume: 10–25 personalized emails/week. Soft-CTA demo emails benchmark 10%+ reply (vs 3.4% cold average); every send/reply/objection is logged — the outreach IS the customer development.
- Description credit is the highest-quality placement in the whole system: the video owner can legally include a clickable link.

## Secondary loop — copy-paste comments (distribution, instrumented experiment)

- Default post-chop success screen = ready-to-paste timestamp block, opening with `0:00` (description-ready if the creator grabs it).
- **Watermark is URL-free**: `⏱ Chapters by YouChop` with 2–3 rotating phrasings. Links get comments auto-held for review (up to 60 days, usually never approved) — a domain in the watermark silently kills the loop.
- **+1 credit on copy** (not on paste — unverifiable). One-line reminder in UI: *"Paste it yourself — never automate comments."*
- Honest math: k ≈ 0.05–0.2 — a compounding CAC-free channel, **not** virality. The real viewer-install engine is Chrome Web Store SEO: keyword title ("YouChop — AI Chapters & Timestamps for YouTube"), review velocity (post-3rd-chop prompt), install velocity.
- **Shutdown switch (pre-committed):** weekly incognito survival check of 10 sampled pasted comments. Survival <60% for two consecutive checks, or ANY creator complaint reaching the brand → watermark goes opt-in, copy credit dies.

## Compliance stance (Decision, 2026-07-02)

- No automated posting, ever — YouTube's spam policy prohibits "high-volume, repetitive, or deceptive comments"; the canonical violation is identical messages across hundreds of videos. Human-pasted, video-specific timestamps are on the safe side; the watermark is the only repeated string, so it stays short, URL-free, and rotated.
- The creator-outreach half of the loop is the safer, higher-value channel and takes priority.
- Extension stays read-only on YouTube's DOM; minimal permissions (youtube.com only); MV3; honest single-purpose CWS listing.

## The adoption paradox (accepted happily)

Every creator who adopts chapters removes that video from the viewer-side inventory. Good: the payoff is the description credit, the pack sale, and the sub — which is the whole point. The viewer product cannibalizes itself into creator revenue.

## Channel order of operations

1. Creator sample outreach (this week, manual).
2. Chrome Web Store SEO (publish, keyword title, review prompt).
3. Copy-paste loop (after the paste-a-URL web fallback ships — the parachute goes on before the jump).
4. Product Hunt launch + r/NewTubers value-post (free tool for creators, no link-drop).
5. Referral credits (+10) — expected 5–15% of installs, minor channel; fraud controls deferred until volume exists.
